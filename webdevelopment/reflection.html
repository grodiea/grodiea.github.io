M&C GRODIE FARMS

Reflection

You been farmin' long?

As soon as he could barely see over the steering wheel, Matt started hauling hay bales in the back of his dad’s rusted out Ford F-150 from the field to the barn on the family farm. Before long, he was behind the wheel of the family tractor. Bailing hay is a slow methodical process so Matt quickly learned how to maneuver the gears and how to switch equipment. While hay season is a time-consuming portion of farmers’ summers, it is only a small portion of their responsibilities.

Alongside his dad, Matt also learned how to care for livestock. At a young age, he learned about life and death. A few times, he needed to aid his dad in the birth of cattle when mother cows were unable to deliver independently. He also witnessed a well-loved cow be euthanized by the veterinarian when she was too sick to recover. He was also present to wrangle squealing newborn piglets needing to get tagged and vaccinated. Nearly every farm experience, Matt was there learning alongside his dad.

In 2012, a farm they previously toured officially became theirs. Shortly after, they changed the name from Lone Elm Farm to M&C Grodie Farms. Starting a family was another dream for Matt and Courtney. Raising children on their own property came was inevitable; because as a third-generation farmer, Matt has farming in his blood.

Matt’s mom bought a precious picture of two young boys, sheepishly looking at one another with the text “you been farmin' long?” One of the young boys resembled her son and she knew Matt's dad dreamt of his son continuing the legacy of farming. That picture hung in the basement of Matt's parent’s home until Matt moved in with Courtney. Little did his mom know that her purchase of this picture nearly 40 years ago would beprominently displayed on the wall of her son’s home; surrounded by acres and acres of farmland. To answer the question: yes, because farming is in our blood.
